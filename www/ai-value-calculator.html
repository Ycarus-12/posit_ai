Leadership has made AI a priority.
Here is what it costs without the right structure.

Building AI tools takes real time from real people. When there is no dedicated function to own that work, it falls on whoever is available, in whatever hours they can spare, with no shared standard and no plan for what happens when things change next month.

These costs are not a reason to avoid AI tooling. They are a reason to own it properly. The right function eliminates the Year 1 spike by absorbing the build. It cuts the ongoing tax by maintaining the portfolio systematically. And because each build informs the next, a dedicated team ships new tools faster every year, not slower.

The question is not whether to build. The question is whether the hidden cost of doing it ad hoc is a plan you are comfortable with.
